Sunset Beach Restoration: Project Progress

Coastal Engineering is leading a comprehensive environmental restoration project at Sunset Beach, focusing on protecting and enhancing the natural ecosystem while improving public access. This project represents our commitment to sustainable coastal development and environmental stewardship.

Phase 1 Reaches 75% Completion

Northern section sand replenishment progress (February 2025)

Phase 1 of the beach restoration project has reached 75% completion. The northern section (Stations 0+00 to 15+00) has been successfully completed, with approximately 45,000 cubic yards of sand placed and graded to design specifications. Wave monitoring stations are now operational and collecting valuable data.

Key Updates

  • Northern section sand replenishment completed
  • Wave monitoring stations installed and collecting data
  • Water quality monitoring shows acceptable parameters
  • Equipment mobilization planning underway for southern section

Next Steps

  • Begin sand placement at Station 15+00 (scheduled for Feb 15)
  • Install remaining wave monitoring stations
  • Conduct pre-construction survey of dune vegetation
  • Complete environmental monitoring report
January 28, 2025

Dune Protection System Implementation

New dune protection system with native vegetation installation

A significant milestone has been achieved with the completion of dune protection measures along the northern section of Sunset Beach. This system will help prevent erosion and protect the restored beach area. Our team is now preparing for the next phase, which includes planting native vegetation to further stabilize the dunes.

January 15, 2025

Environmental Assessment Results

Environmental scientists conducting wildlife impact assessment

The comprehensive environmental impact assessment has been completed, confirming that our restoration methods are achieving their intended goals while maintaining minimal impact on local wildlife. This validation allows us to proceed confidently with our restoration timeline.

Key Features of the Project

  • 2.5 miles of coastline restoration
  • Native dune vegetation integration
  • Advanced erosion control systems
  • Enhanced public access points
  • Wildlife habitat preservation
  • Modern stormwater management
  • Educational outreach elements
